About Me

Hi, I'm Dhanasankar K, an enthusiastic and dedicated Electronics and Communication Engineering graduate (B.E., Class of 2025) with a deep-rooted passion for digital system design at the hardware level. My academic journey and hands-on projects have shaped a strong foundation in digital electronics, with a particular interest in Verilog-based RTL design, FPGA implementation, and real-time system simulation. I am highly motivated by the challenge of solving real-world problems through precise, bit-level control, and I enjoy the intricacies of developing optimized and reliable hardware logic.

Currently, I am undergoing advanced Design Verification (DV) training at Silicon Craft VLSI Training & Research Institute, Chennai, where I am expanding my expertise in Verilog, SystemVerilog, and the Universal Verification Methodology (UVM). My current focus is on mastering SystemVerilog for functional verification, developing constrained-random environments, and gaining exposure to protocol-level testing — all of which are driving me toward a successful career in the semiconductor and VLSI design domain. With a combination of theoretical knowledge and practical experience, I am eager to contribute to cutting-edge digital hardware design and verification projects.

Areas of Passion

  • RTL Design & Digital Logic Architecture – Designing efficient combinational and sequential logic using Verilog/SystemVerilog.
  • FPGA System Integration – Deploying real-time systems on Artix-7 and DE2-70 boards with effective I/O handling.
  • Functional Verification & Testbench Creation – Writing SystemVerilog/UVM-based reusable environments with assertions and coverage.
  • Digital Protocol Implementation – Designing and verifying UART, SPI, I2C, APB, and bridge modules like APB to I2C.
  • Timing Closure & Clock Domain Crossing – Managing multicycle paths and CDC for successful synthesis using Vivado.
  • Image Processing on FPGA – Creating pixel-based filters (e.g., Sobel) and visual pipelines using Verilog RTL.
  • EDA Tool Proficiency – Using Icarus Verilog, GTKWave, and Xilinx Vivado for simulation and synthesis workflows.
  • Low-Power RTL Techniques – Exploring gated clocks, minimal toggling, and area-efficient digital designs.
  • Learning & Innovation in VLSI – Enthusiastic about building projects, learning new methodologies, and solving bit-level hardware challenges.
